Privacy Policy

April 6, 2026

Story Reader is an Android EPUB reading application. This policy explains what information the app stores, which optional third-party services it can connect to, and what information is not collected by the developer.

Summary

  • Story Reader does not require a developer-run account.
  • Story Reader does not sell personal data.
  • Story Reader does not include advertising.
  • Story Reader does not send your reading data to a developer-operated server.
  • Optional sync features use cloud services you explicitly choose and configure.

Information stored on your device

Story Reader may store the following information locally on your device:

  • Imported books and related metadata, such as title, author, and cover art.
  • Your reading position and reading progress.
  • Reading statistics, such as reading sessions and word-count progress.
  • App preferences, including reader settings and text-to-speech settings.
  • Credentials for optional sync providers, stored using Android security features when available.

Optional cloud services

Story Reader can connect to third-party services only when you choose to enable them.

  • Google Drive: if you connect Google Drive, Story Reader can store sync backup data in your Google Drive app-specific storage area.
  • Nextcloud: if you configure Nextcloud sync, Story Reader can store sync backup data in your Nextcloud account.
  • OPDS or other book sources: if you connect a catalog or download source, Story Reader may use the URL and credentials you provide in order to browse or download books from that source.

What the developer does not collect

The developer does not operate a central service that collects your reading data. In particular:

  • No developer-run user account is required to use the app.
  • No reading progress is uploaded to a developer-owned server.
  • No analytics or advertising SDK is intentionally used to profile you for ads.

How cloud data is handled

When you enable an optional sync feature, the app transfers data directly between your device and the third-party service you selected. Your use of those services is also governed by their own privacy policies and terms.

Data retention and control

You can control your data in several ways:

  • Remove books and app data from your device.
  • Disconnect Google Drive or Nextcloud from inside the app.
  • Delete backup data from the cloud service you use.
  • Uninstall the app from your device.

Children’s privacy

Story Reader is not specifically directed to children. The developer does not knowingly collect personal information from children through a developer-operated backend service.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated as the app evolves. If the policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page.

Contact

For support or privacy questions, please use the project repository: github.com/jalbertcory/story-reader/issues
